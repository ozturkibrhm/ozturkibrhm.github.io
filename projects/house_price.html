House Price Prediction with Advanced Regression Techniques

  • Tech Stack: Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn, TensorFlow/Keras
  • Kaggle URL: Project Link

This project was built for the Kaggle challenge House Prices – Advanced Regression Techniques. The objective was to predict SalePrice from a dataset with 80 property features. I handled the full pipeline: EDA, data cleaning, feature engineering, and modeling with both classic ML and a small ANN.

Data Pipeline

  • EDA: Distribution checks, target skew/kurtosis, correlation heatmaps, outlier inspection.
  • Missing Values: Custom strategies (e.g., fill with "None", median imputation, or drop when appropriate).
  • Categorical Encoding: Label Encoding for ordinal-like fields; One-Hot Encoding for nominal features.
  • Numerical Fixes: Box-Cox transform for skewed variables; engineered TotalSF and similar aggregates.
  • Feature Importance: Yellowbrick Rank1D to highlight top predictive variables.

Modeling

  • Traditional ML: Baselines with regularized regressors and cross-validation.
  • Neural Network (Keras): Dense layers with Dropout and L2 regularization; EarlyStopping & ReduceLROnPlateau for stable training.
  • Metrics: RMSE, MAE, and R² on a held-out validation split.

The final solution produced generalizable validation scores and generated a Kaggle-ready submission.csv with test predictions.
